# Cardiovascular Health Guidelines - Clinical Reference

## 1. Blood Pressure Categories (Resting BP - trestbps)
- **Normal**: Less than 120/80 mmHg.
- **Elevated**: Systolic between 120-129 AND diastolic less than 80.
- **Hypertension Stage 1**: Systolic 130-139 OR diastolic 80-89.
- **Hypertension Stage 2**: Systolic 140 or higher OR diastolic 90 or higher.
- **Hypertensive Crisis**: Systolic higher than 180 AND/OR diastolic higher than 120.

## 2. Cholesterol Levels (Serum Cholesterol - chol)
- **Desirable**: Less than 200 mg/dL.
- **Borderline High**: 200–239 mg/dL.
- **High**: 240 mg/dL and above.

## 3. Fasting Blood Sugar (fbs)
- **Normal**: Less than 100 mg/dL.
- **Prediabetes**: 100 to 125 mg/dL.
- **Diabetes**: 126 mg/dL or higher.
- *Note: Clinical indicator 'fbs > 120' in the dataset suggests high risk.*

## 4. Heart Rate (thalach)
- **Normal Resting HR**: 60 to 100 beats per minute.
- **Target Heart Rate (Exercise)**: Generally estimated as (220 - age). Max heart rate achieved (thalach) is a key indicator of cardiovascular fitness.

## 5. Chest Pain Types (cp)
- **Type 0 (Typical Angina)**: Heavy pressure, tightness, or squeezing in the chest. Usually triggered by exertion.
- **Type 1 (Atypical Angina)**: Chest pain that doesn't meet all typical criteria but is still concerning.
- **Type 2 (Non-anginal Pain)**: Sharp or stabbing pain, often related to breathing or position, less likely to be heart-related.
- **Type 3 (Asymptomatic)**: No chest pain reported, but other diagnostic factors may be present.

## 6. Preventive Recommendations
- **Diet**: Increase intake of vegetables, fruits, whole grains, and lean proteins. Limit sodium, saturated fats, and added sugars.
- **Exercise**: At least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic activity per week (e.g., brisk walking).
- **Smoking**: Immediate cessation is the single most effective way to reduce cardiovascular risk.
- **Stress**: Manage stress through meditation, deep breathing, or physical activity.
- **Monitoring**: Regular check-ups for blood pressure and cholesterol are essential.

## 7. Disclaimer
These guidelines are based on standard clinical references (ACC/AHA) and are for educational purposes. Any clinical decisions must be made by a qualified healthcare professional.
